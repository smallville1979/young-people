💰 Money, Benefits & Student Finance

Tom says: "Nobody tells young people they can claim UC. Nobody explains student finance properly. You're leaving money on the table every month. Stop that."

Universal Credit for Young People

If you're 18+ and on low income (even working part-time), you can claim UC. Students can claim in some circumstances — especially in summer or if you have a disability or caring responsibilities.

  • Check UC eligibility → Official
  • 18-21? You can still claim — don't let anyone tell you otherwise
  • Working part-time? UC tops up low wages — apply anyway
  • Summer holidays from uni? Claim in the gaps if you're not earning
  • Disabled or chronic illness? Check PIP too — gov.uk/pip →

Student Finance

  • Student Finance England → Official
  • Maintenance loan goes up if your parents earn less — check your actual entitlement
  • Wales and Scotland have maintenance grants that don't need to be paid back
  • Hardship fund — your uni has one. Ask Student Services. Most don't advertise it
  • Full-time student? You don't pay council tax — claim the exemption →

💼 Jobs, Work & Getting Paid

Best Part-Time Jobs for Students

  • Retail & hospitality — flexible, tips, easy to get and quick to start
  • University campus jobs — library assistant, reception, student ambassador. Ask Student Services
  • Tutoring — if you're good at any subject, £15-25/hr tutoring younger students. Post on Facebook groups or Tutorful →
  • Gig work — Deliveroo, Amazon Flex, DPD — set your own hours around lectures
  • Call centres — often recruit students specifically for evening and weekend shifts

Uni & College Life — Use What You're Paying For

  • Student Union — free events, cheap bar, clubs and societies. Most are free to join. It's already paid for in your fees
  • Societies — film club, debate, hiking, gaming, cooking — go to freshers' fair and pick two
  • Uni gym — usually £5-10/month vs £30+ commercial. One of the best perks
  • Public lectures and open events — universities run free public events all year. Check the website

Free Days Out (No Excuses)

  • 🏛️ National museums and galleries — British Museum, Tate Modern, V&A, Natural History Museum, Science Museum — all free entry
  • 🌳 Parkrun — free 5k every Saturday morning, everywhere in the UK. parkrun.org.uk →
  • 📚 Library — free wi-fi, books, films to borrow, free events. Most people forget about it
  • 🎵 Open mic nights — free entry everywhere. Great atmosphere, no spend required

🏳️‍🌈 Community, Friends & Belonging

Tom says: "Moving somewhere new and knowing nobody is hard. It happens to everyone. There are groups for literally everything. You just have to show up once — the second time is easy."

LGBTQ+ Groups & Support

  • Queers of the North — Newcastle LGBTQ+ community group, regular socials and safe spaces. Search on Facebook and Instagram for your nearest chapter
  • The Open Chair — LGBTQ+ social café format running in various Northern cities. Google "[your city] Open Chair" to find events
  • LGBT Foundation — lgbt.foundation → UK-wide helpline, resources and local groups directory
  • Local Pride events — Google "[your city] Pride" for annual events and year-round social groups attached to them
  • LGBTQ+ Society at your uni — safe space, events, social nights. Your SU will have one, it's free to join

Book Clubs & Community Groups

  • Library book clubs — free, local, relaxed. Ask at your library — most run them monthly and genuinely want new members
  • Meetup reading groups — search "book club" on meetup.com → — loads in every major city
  • Reddit local subs — r/[YourCity] for recommendations, local events and genuinely useful answers
  • Nextdoor — local community app. Free stuff, neighbourhood events, local groups
Real talk about making friends: The single best thing you can do is show up to one thing you're slightly nervous about. Meetup groups are full of people in exactly the same boat. Most people are just waiting for someone else to make the first move. Be that person once.

💑 Dating & Going Out — Broke But Still Having a Life

Tom says: "You don't need to spend £100 on a first date. A decent conversation in a park costs nothing. People are more impressed by confidence and creativity than an overpriced restaurant."

Free & Cheap Date Ideas That Actually Work

☕ Coffee Walk

£2-3 takeaway coffee and a walk. Low pressure, easy to talk, you can leave when you want.

🏛️ Museum Date

Free entry. Loads to talk about. You look genuinely interesting. Hard to go wrong.

🎭 Comedy / Open Mic Night

Free or cheap entry. You're laughing together from the start — best date atmosphere possible.

🍕 Cook Together

Home cooking for two costs £8-12. Better than any restaurant and way more impressive.

Dating Apps — The Real Picture

  • Hinge — most popular for genuine relationships in UK right now. Free version works fine, don't pay
  • Bumble — women message first. Filters out a lot of nonsense for women users
  • Tinder — still works, especially in student cities where there's a big user base
  • LGBTQ+: Grindr (gay/bi men), HER (women/non-binary), Scruff, Feeld for open relationships
  • ⚠️ Never pay for premium on dating apps — the free versions are enough when you're starting out. Don't waste money on it

Going Out Cheap

  • Student nights — Mon/Wed nights are cheap at most student union bars. Under £5 in most places
  • Guest list — most clubs have free entry before a time on guest list. Message the promoter on Instagram a day before
  • Pre-drinks at home — obvious but it works. Half your mates are doing it anyway
  • Wetherspoons — cheap drinks, no dress code, all ages, no judgement. Good for big groups
  • Gig tickets — Skiddle and Dice have loads of local gigs under £10. skiddle.com →
